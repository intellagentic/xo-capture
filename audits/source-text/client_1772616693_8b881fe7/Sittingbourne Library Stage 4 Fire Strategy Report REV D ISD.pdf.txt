1.4. THE PROPERTY SUMMARY  
1.4.1. Sittingbourne Library, Central Avenue, Sittingbourne, Kent, ME10 4AH is a single-storey public 
library building. The accommodation comprises a main public library area with associated 
ancillary rooms including offices, staff areas, WCs, stores, consultatio n/group rooms and a 
bin store, as shown on the proposed drawing set (A.04). 
1.4.2. The building is single-storey, non-residential and under 2,000m². It is accessed by members 
of the public and staff. The fire risk profile is considered low to moderate, reflecting typical 
combustible load associated with library materials and variable public occupancy. 
1.5. DESCRIPTION OF PROPOSED WORKS – SITTINGBOURNE LIBRARY 
1.5.1. From a fire engineering perspective, the proposed refurbishment of Sittingbourne Library 
has been developed to achieve compliance with the Building Regulations 2010, with 
particular regard to Approved Document B (Fire Safety), Approved Document F (Ventilation) 
and Approved Document M (Access to and Use of Buildings), together with relevant British

Standards. The works relate to a single -storey, non-residential public building comprising 
library accommodation with associated offices, consultation rooms, staff areas and 
sanitary facilities. The key fire safety provisions incorporated within the design  are 
summarised below. 
a. Fire Detection and Alarm System:  A Category L2 automatic fire detection and alarm 
system is proposed in accordance with BS 5839 -1, providing automatic detection to 
escape routes, rooms opening onto escape routes, and identified higher -risk areas, to 
ensure early warning and support a simultaneous evacuation strategy. 
b. Means of Escape: Means of escape are provided directly to open air via multiple final 
exits distributed around the building perimeter. Primary escape routes discharge 
towards the principal entrance, with secondary exits provided to alternative elevations. 
Final exit doors are fitted with simple fastenings and panic hardware where required. 
Travel distances and exit capacities have been assessed against Approved Document 
B (Volume 2) and are demonstrated on the accompanying fire strategy drawings. 
c. Structural Fire Protection and Compartmentation:  New internal partitions are formed 
using fire-resisting metal stud constructions (e.g. Type A206015) achieving a minimum 
of 60 minutes fire resistance in integrity and insulation. Fire -resisting construction is 
provided where necessary to enclose higher -risk rooms and to limit fire and smoke 
spread, while the principal open-plan library areas are treated as a single compartment 
in accordance with ADB guidance for buildings of this scale. 
d. Doors and Glazing:  Fire-resisting doors are provided where required to protect fire -
resisting enclosures, incorporating vision panels and simple fastenings to support safe 
egress. Final exit doors are arranged to avoid trip hazards and to maintain accessibility. 
Glazing within doors and critical locations is safety glazing compliant with BS 6206, with 
manifestation and guarding provided as necessary in accordance with Approved 
Document K.
