1.0 INTRODUCTION
1.1. PURPOSE OF REPORT
1.1.1. Bennington Green Limited has been instructed to prepare this Fire Strategy Report for
Tringham House, 580 Deansleigh Road, Bournemouth, Dorset, BH7 7DT. The report sets out
the fire safety strategy for the proposed development and demonstrates how the design is
intended to satisfy the functional requirements of Schedule 1, Part B (Fire Safety) of the
Building Regulations 2010 (as amended).

1.1.2. The purpose of this report is to define the overarching fire safety design principles applicable
to the building and to confirm the basis upon which compliance with the Building
Regulations has been assessed. The strategy provides a coordinated framework for fire
safety provisions including means of escape, compartmentation, structural fire protection,
fire detection and alarm systems, fire-fighting facilities, and fire safety management
arrangements.

1.1.7. This Fire Strategy has been prepared based on the information available at the time of
writing and reflects the design intent of the building at the current stage of development.
The strategy is therefore intended to support the coordination of the architectural, structural
and building services design and to inform the preparation of fire strategy drawings and
specifications. Should material alterations be made to the building layout, occupancy
arrangements, or fire protection systems during the detailed design or construction stages,
the fire strategy should be reviewed and updated accordingly to ensure continued
compliance with the relevant fire safety requirements.
1.2. SCOPE OF THIS FIRE STRATEGY
1.2.1. This Fire Strategy Report addresses the proposed internal alteration works only, which
comprise fit-out and refurbishment primarily to the Ground, First and Second Floors. The
Third Floor and all existing vertical circulation (including the three protected stairs), final
exits, and external elements remain unchanged.
1.2.2. The report demonstrates that the proposed internal works will not make the building more
unsatisfactory in relation to the functional requirements of Part B of Schedule 1 to the Building
Regulations 2010 (as amended) than it was before the works. It does not constitute a full
retrospective fire strategy for the entire existing building. Where existing elements are
described, this is solely to provide context for the impact (or lack of impact) of the proposed
internal alterations. Ongoing compliance with the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order
2005, including any remedial fire-stopping works and the preparation of a full Fire Risk
Assessment once the building is occupied, remains the responsibility of the Responsible
Person.
