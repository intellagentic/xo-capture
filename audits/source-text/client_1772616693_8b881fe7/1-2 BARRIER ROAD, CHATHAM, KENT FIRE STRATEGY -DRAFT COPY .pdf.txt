2.0 PURPOSE OF REPORT  
The purpose of this report is to set out the fire safety principles for agreement  with the Statutory 
Authorities. Within this document, the proposed solutions for means of escape, internal and  
external fire spread issues as well as firefighting access and facilities are outlined. The aspects 
of design that do  not follow recognised statutory guidance have been highlighted within this fire 
strategy document and may require  further consideration by the design team , and further 
discussions with the Approving ADB Volume 2, 2022 requirement  
 
3.0 BUILDING SUMMARY  
The proposed building will comprise a ground floor and a first-floor storey building, to which will 
be provided with two escape stairs . The height of the building from the ground level to the 
uppermost story is approximately 5 meters . 
 
The building will be used as a crisis facility for mental patients and will be operated  by 
healthcare  workers. 
 
The home will provide 6 Bedrooms in total with 1 bedroom on the ground floor and 5 bedrooms 
on the 2 nd floor. 
 
A central communal wing will house ancillary accommodation such as kitchen, staff rooms, 
laundry and offices. 
 
Due to the fact that the building will operate as a crisis home for mental patients, it is more 
likely that  residents will have a cognitive impairment, emotional stress , behavioural issues and 
lack of self-care therefore, the recommendations detailed in sections 2.33 to 2.46 of ADB 
covering residential care homes have been adopted wherever possible.   As well HTM Health 
Technical Memorandum 05 -02: Fire code Guidance in support of functional provisions (Fire 
safety in the design of healthcare premises)  2015 2.40 -2.68

Specific fire safety information relating to the above premises  
 
Although the range of services provided varies considerably, there are common issues that must 
be considered to enable appropriate levels of fire safety to be achieved. The ultimate aim is to 
provide a safe and secure environment where patients can receive  care and treatment; however, 
safety from the effects of fire and maintaining the required levels of security are equally 
important, and the design of fire precautions and evacuation strategies should not compromise 
security.  
 
Highly trained specialised nursing and clinical staff are always present when the premises are 
occupied, and they will be trained to take the lead role in the evacuation of patients.

number of staff available will influence the speed of evacuation. Such evacuation may be 
progressive horizontal evacuation to other compartments or sub -compartments as described in
