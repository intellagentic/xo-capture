Hi Alan,
Following our recent meeting, please find below the prioritized list of regulations and standards for ingestion into the trial
system. This list is structured to reflect how the software compliance engine should be organized, starting with core statutory
guidance followed by specific engineering and performance-based standards.
1. Core Statutory Guidance (Building Regulations)
These should sit at the top level of the software compliance engine.
  - Building Regulations – England: Building Regulations 2010 (as amended), The Building Safety Act 2022, and Building
(Higher-Risk Buildings Procedures) Regulations 2023.
  - Approved Documents (England):
      - Approved Document B (Fire Safety) – Volume 1: Dwellings & Volume 2: Buildings other than dwellings.
      - Approved Document 7 – Materials and Workmanship.
      - Approved Document M – Access to and use of buildings.
      - Approved Document F – Ventilation.
      - Approved Document L – Conservation of fuel and power.
      - Approved Document K – Protection from falling.
2. Key Approved Document B (ADB) Sections & Tables
  - Sections: B1 (Means of warning and escape), B2 (Internal fire spread - linings), B3 (Internal fire spread - structure), B4
(External fire spread), and B5 (Firefighting access and facilities).
  - Tables/Diagrams for Automation: Tables 2.1, 2.3, 3.1–3.2, and B4; Diagrams 2.1–2.10; and Appendices B, C, and D.
3. Fire Engineering & Performance-Based Design
  - BS 7974 and the PD 7974 series (Parts 1 through 7) covering fire development, smoke movement, structural response,
detection, intervention, human factors, and risk assessment.
4. Means of Escape & Occupant Movement
  - BS 9999:2017/2024 (Design and management).
  - BS 9991:2024 (Residential).
  - BS ISO 16738 and BS ISO 20414 (Evacuation modelling and validation).
  - SFPE Handbook of Fire Protection Engineering.
5. Specialized Systems & Compliance
  - Detection and Alarms: BS 5839 (Parts 1 & 6) and the BS EN 54 series.
  - Smoke Control: BS EN 12101 series, BS 7346, and BS 9999 smoke ventilation sections.
  - Suppression: BS EN 12845, BS 9251 (Sprinklers), BS 8458 (Water mist), and BS EN 15004 (Gas).
  - Emergency Lighting: BS 5266-1, BS EN 1838, and BS EN 50172.
  - Resistance & Testing: BS EN 13501, BS EN 1363, BS EN 1634-1 (Doors), and BS EN 1366 (Fire stopping).
  - External Walls: PAS 9980:2022, BS 8414, and BR 135.
  - Structural Fire Engineering: Eurocodes EN 1991, 1992, 1993, and 1995 (Part 1-2 for each).
  - Healthcare: HTM 05-01, 05-02, and 05-03.
6. Suggested Software Library Structure
We recommend a module-based structure similar to that used by firms like Arup or Buro Happold:
01_Regulations | 02_ADB_Compliance | 03_Escape_Calculations | 04_Fire_Alarm_Design | 
05_Smoke_Control |
